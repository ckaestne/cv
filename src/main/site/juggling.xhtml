Juggling in Pittsburgh

Where to juggle in Pittsburgh (last updated 2018-09-30):

  • Carnegie Mellon Masters of Flying Objects
    Meeting Sundays 3-5pm and Thursdays evening on or near the CMU campus. In nice summer weather, Thursday meetings are typically outside on the cut in front of Purnell and Sunday meetings are on Schenley Plaza in front of the Carnegie Library; in the winter and in bad weather the Sunday meeting is the atrium of Newell Simon Hall and the midweek meeting is in a changing location on campus. Growing group of students and nonstudents. See news section on the website or join mailing list for location announcements and short-term updates.
    website, mailing list, map
  • Pittsburgh Show Offs
    No longer meeting separately. Now joined with the CMU juggling club on the CMU campus (see above).
    website, facebook
  • The Steel City Clown Brigade
    Clown troup with lots of children who do also some juggling. Performing frequently in Hospitals and for various events. Meeting in Homestead, PA. See website for meeting times.
    website
  • Leave It To Beaver Valley Jugglers
    Jugglers meeting about 45min outside Pittsburgh in Baden, PA (Villa St. Joseph, Heritage Hall). Small group meeting regularly Sundays 3-5:30pm. Organizers of the now defunct Not Quite Pittsburgh Juggling Festival.
    website, map
  • Other circusy meetings
    THICK bikes organizes a weekly unicycling meeting at the South Side near the Hoffbraeuhaus, Friday 6-8pmi (indoor alternative in the winter). Slacklining and Acrobatics/AcroYoga tend to happen on Sunday afternoon in Schenley Plaza; the Pittsburgh Flow Collective meets in the evening nearby in Schenley Park. Aerial classes are offered by Iron City Circus and Pittsburgh Aerial Silks. The Pittsburgh Circus Arts Collaborative offers performances...

If you know other regular meetings, let me know about it (mail: kaestner (at) cmu.edu). I don't run any of the clubs, I just wanted to make them easier to find. If you're looking for a performer, please email someone else, perhaps a club organizer. Inspired by Iain Murray's site.
